More About Unsupervised Learning

Written By Ved Panse Last Modified: 20 May 2022

Unsupervised Learning does not make use of historic data (labeled data). Instead, it is inspired by the patterns in data. These patterns can be co-relations between two features of the data, or the periodicity of the function.

Advantages

Unsupervised learning does not need human supervision and is self-complete.

A disadvantage

There is no way to check the accuracy of unsupervised learning.

Clustering

Clustering is somewhat similar to classification (in supervised learning). Elements can be classified into multiple 'clusters' based on multiple factors (as opposed to only two factors in classification).

Image from https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/proxy/1*nykOoMQpu9GiODUU48Aesg.png

One of the most ubiquitous use cases is fake news detection algorithms. When a piece of news contains most of the factors in alignment with those of fake news, that piece of news is associated with fake news.

Dimensional Reduction

As the name suggests, this process reduces some of the data. More particularly, it reduces the redundant information.

For example, emails get filtered automatically into spam and non-spam folders. If you receive an email that has 4 factors in common with other spam emails but only 1 factor in common with non-spam emails, it will get redirected to the spam folder.
